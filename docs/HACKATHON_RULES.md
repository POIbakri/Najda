# Hackathon Rules & Context (Tatweer Hackathon)

*Distilled from the official participant handbook for agent use. The original handbook is the source of truth — keep a copy at `docs/OFFICIAL_HANDBOOK.md` if available, and defer to it on any conflict.*

## The event
A civic-tech hackathon run with Abu Dhabi University. The mission: rural UAE communities can't access the digital services city residents take for granted; build the reusable tools that close that gap. The pilot community is **Al Qua'a** — a remote desert locality ~90 minutes from Abu Dhabi toward Al Ain. It is a **camel-farming** community, sits on the **Tropic of Cancer**, and is renowned for **stargazing** (very low light pollution). "The best projects feel built for this place and its people."

Implications the build must respect: no street addresses, dispersed settlements, patchy connectivity, harsh sunlight (outdoor use), Arabic-first users plus a farm-labour population (Urdu/Hindi), and lower digital literacy among some users.

## Submission rules (the GitHub requirement)
- Each team submits **one public GitHub repository**.
- The project must be **open-source** (include a LICENSE — we use MIT).
- **Only commits before the Saturday 8:00 PM GST deadline count.** Commit continuously and timestamp your work in-window.
- **AI and all other tools are permitted, with no restrictions.**
- Round 1 is judged **entirely from the repository** — there is no demo until the top 10. The README and repo contents are the submission.

## Team rules
- Teams up to 5; **solo is allowed** (this is a solo build).
- Participants must be 18+.

## The five challenges
1. **Taking the first entrepreneurial step** — help a first-time founder go from idea to first concrete action (action plan, licences/costs, first resource or customer).
2. **Reaching people quickly across a dispersed community** — precise location sharing and routing the nearest help; the first minutes before formal help arrives. ← **OUR CHALLENGE**
3. **The data gap for local entrepreneurs** — local market/demand data and lightweight research for people deciding what to build.
4. **Connecting residents to services, opportunities and events** — discovery and notification of local services/opportunities/events.
5. **Free choice** — an open lane.

## Why we chose Challenge 2
- It **generates its own data** (a button press captures GPS), so it doesn't depend on external datasets we can't obtain in the time.
- Highest **Impact** ceiling (life-safety, a pressing need, benefit to the whole dispersed community) — Impact is the #1 criterion and the first tiebreak.
- **Feasibility** is strong when scoped as a *community layer* (low cost, any phone, no government integration required).
- **Falsifiability** is winnable via a measurable drill — the criterion most teams fail (see `docs/EVIDENCE.md`). The national EMS provider itself names *precise location* and *language barriers* as its top obstacles — exactly what Najda addresses.

The scope guardrail (community first-response layer, not an EMS replacement) is mandatory — see `docs/PRODUCT_AND_UX.md` and `docs/JUDGING_AND_POINTS.md`.
