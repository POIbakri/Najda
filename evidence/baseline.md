# EMS response-time baseline (cited)

Najda compresses the **first-minutes window** before formal EMS arrives. The gap
it targets is real and documented.

## Urban UAE — fast
- **Dubai (DCAS):** record average response **~7.5 minutes** in 2023.
  — Dubai Media Office, 2023.
- **Northern Emirates (National Ambulance):** average brought to **~8.5 minutes**,
  down from ~18 minutes in 2014. — Khaleej Times / National Ambulance, 2019.

## Remote villages — slow
- **Ghalilah** (~30 km from Ras Al Khaimah city): residents reported waits **up to
  60 minutes**. — *The National*, 2021.
- **Wadi Kub** (~40 km out): **30–40 minutes**. — *The National*, 2021.

## The obstacle Najda targets
The national EMS provider names its top obstacles as **precise location,
changing/absent street names, and language barriers** — precisely what Najda's
address-free locator (~1–2 m, [`locator-accuracy.md`](./locator-accuracy.md))
and Arabic/English/Urdu UI address.

## Framing
Urban **7.5–8.5 min** vs remote villages **30–60 min**. Najda does not replace
the ambulance — it puts a **trained neighbour** on scene during the first
minutes of that 30–60 minute gap, and hands a precise location to the 998
operator so the ambulance arrives faster too.
